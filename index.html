Intro

Hi, I'm Noah. Welcome to my data science portfolio. This is a collection of the individual and team data science work I have completed over the past few years.

I graduated from UC-Berkeley in 2021 with a double major in data science and economics. Currently I am pursuing my Masters in Data Science, completing my degree in the spring of 2024. In addition to grad school, I work as a data scientist in Samsung's Corporate Strategy Department.

I have interest an interest in machine learning, specifically how LLMs can change big tech and finance adjacent industries. Other fields of data science that interest me are systems engineering, deep learning, time series forecasting and data vizualisation.

In my free time, I root for my sports teams, particularly as a devoted fan of Cal football, the Warriors, the 49ers, and Manchester United. Additionally, I love to explore the world through travel, with my most recent trip being to London and Paris.

Work

Stock identification and Prediction from Financial News

A Natural Language Processing (NLP) project in the financial domain, focusing on identifying stock(s) in financial news and predicting the positive/negative/neutral sentiment of the stock(s). Various state-of-the-art machine learning approaches, including RoBERTa, T5 generative model, CRF method, and NER-BIO tagging schemes were implemented. Through rigorous experimentation, a two-step method that first extracts entities and then conducts sentiment analysis emerged as the most effective, achieving an accuracy of 76.14%.

House Price Prediction API and Deployment

The project involved the development of a Python prediction API using FastAPI, which was deployed on Kubernetes and hosted on Azure AKS (Azure Kubernetes Service). Several key accomplishments of the project include the utilization of Redis for input caching, resulting in a substantial 38% reduction in prediction latency during load tests. These load tests involved up to 10 virtual users and 15,000 iterations. In addition, the project implementing a multistage Docker image build process, leading to a 30% reduction in the image size.

BigData Investment Thesis & Tableau

This project required wrangling 10M+ lines of synthetic restaurant transactions to make a hypothetical investment decision. Deliverables included a jupyter notebook, a Tableau dashboard, finanical models in excel, and a formal 14 page writeup.

Predicting A Movie's success pre-Release: An ML approach

In a machine learning group project focused on predicting a movie's success on Rotten Tomatoes, BERT and LSTM methodologies were used to surpass the logistic baseline by 20%. The model, uses SHAP analysis to explain important features and address fairness concerns. Our final model ultimately exhibited a 90% accuracy rate in forecasting a movie's success prior to its release.

C02 in the Atmosphere: Time Series Forecasts

As part of our Statistical Methods for Time Series and Panel Data course project in R, our group conducted a comprehensive analysis on global CO2 concentration trends. Leveraging time-series methods like we delved into the Kneeling Curve and NOAA CO2 Concentration data, aiming to model and forecast future CO2 concentrations, while also extracting insights into global energy trends and forecasting fossil fuel prices. Our approach encompassed Seasonal ARIMA, STL Decomposition, and Exponential Smoothing techniques.
